A celebration dish that tastes like the best dhaba meal. The chole gets depth from tea, amchur, and whole spices while the bhature puff in minutes.

## Ingredients
- 1.5 cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
- 2 tea bags
- 2 onions, finely chopped
- 2 tomatoes, pureed
- 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
- Whole spices: bay leaf, black cardamom, cloves, cinnamon
- 2 tsp chole masala
- 1 tsp amchur
- 1 tsp garam masala
- 3 tbsp oil
- Salt to taste
- For bhature: 2 cups maida, 3 tbsp yogurt, 1 tsp sugar, 1/2 tsp baking powder, water, salt

## Steps
1. Pressure cook soaked chickpeas with tea bags and salt until soft; discard tea bags.
2. Sauté onions in oil with whole spices until golden. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook off raw smell.
3. Add tomato puree and chole masala; cook until oil separates.
4. Add chickpeas with cooking liquid, simmer 20 minutes, finish with amchur and garam masala.
5. For bhature, knead ingredients into a soft dough, rest 1 hour, roll and deep-fry until puffed.

## Tips
- Add a spoon of black tea reduction if you prefer a darker gravy.
- Serve with pickled onions and green chutney for brightness.
